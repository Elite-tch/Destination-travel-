Hagia Sophia :

The Hagia Sophia, a former cathedral and mosque, is now a museum showcasing Byzantine and Ottoman architecture. It is one of Istanbul's most iconic landmarks.

Details :

  • Location: Sultanahmet Square, Istanbul
  • Opening Hours: 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM (Closed on Mondays)
  • Admission Fee: $20 for adults, $10 for children

Blue Mosque (Sultan Ahmed Mosque) :

The Blue Mosque is known for its stunning architecture, intricate tilework, and six minarets. It is one of Istanbul's most important religious sites.

Details :

  • Location: Sultanahmet Square, Istanbul
  • Opening Hours: 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM (Open Daily)
  • Admission Fee: Free Admission

Grand Bazaar (Kapalıçarşı) :

A bustling covered market spanning over 4,000 shops, offering a diverse array of goods and a vibrant atmosphere for haggling and exploration.

Details :

  • Location: Beyazıt, Fatih, Istanbul
  • Opening Hours: 9:00 AM - 7:00 PM (Closed on Sundays)
  • Admission Fee: Free Admission

Bosphorus Cruise :

Dependent on tour operators Enjoy stunning views of Istanbul's skyline and iconic landmarks from the water on a Bosphorus Cruise, offering a relaxing and memorable way to experience the city's unique blend of European and Asian cultures.

Details :

  • Location: Departure from various points along the Bosphorus Strait
  • Opening Hours: 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM (Closed on Mondays)
  • Admission Fee: $20 for adults, $10 for children
